Common Time Zone Comparisons

EST vs PST (Eastern vs Pacific)

The United States spans multiple time zones. EST (UTC-5) is 3 hours ahead of PST (UTC-8). This is the most common comparison for US-based remote teams. When New York starts work at 9 AM, it's only 6 AM in Los Angeles.

GMT vs IST (London vs India)

IST (UTC+5:30) is 5 hours 30 minutes ahead of GMT (UTC+0). This is critical for UK-India business teams. The best meeting window is typically 1:30 PM - 6:30 PM IST / 8:00 AM - 1:00 PM GMT.

Compare GMT and IST live →

EST vs GMT (New York vs London)

GMT (UTC+0) is 5 hours ahead of EST (UTC-5). During US daylight saving time, the gap narrows to 4 hours. The common meeting window is 9 AM - 12 PM EST / 2 PM - 5 PM GMT.

Compare EST and GMT live →

PST vs JST (San Francisco vs Tokyo)

JST (UTC+9) is 17 hours ahead of PST (UTC-8). Scheduling across the Pacific is challenging — the best overlap is early morning PST / late evening JST, or late afternoon PST / morning JST the next day.

Compare PST and JST live →

CET vs EST (Central Europe vs Eastern US)

CET (UTC+1) is 6 hours ahead of EST (UTC-5). European teams starting at 9 AM CET can catch US colleagues from 9 AM - 12 PM EST (3 PM - 6 PM CET).

Compare CET and EST live →

AEST vs GMT (Sydney vs London)

AEST (UTC+10) is 10 hours ahead of GMT (UTC+0). UK-Australia meetings often happen early morning AEST / late evening GMT, or late afternoon AEST / early morning GMT.

SGT vs EST (Singapore vs New York)

SGT (UTC+8) is 13 hours ahead of EST (UTC-5). The overlap window is narrow: 8 PM - 11 PM SGT / 7 AM - 10 AM EST works for some teams.

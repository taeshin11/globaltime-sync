How to Use GlobalTime Sync

GlobalTime Sync is designed to be intuitive — most users figure it out within seconds. But if you want to get the most out of every feature, here's a complete walkthrough.

Step 1: Search and Add Cities

Start by typing a city name, country, or IANA time zone identifier into the search bar at the top of the dashboard. For example, you can type:

  • "Tokyo" — finds Asia/Tokyo
  • "New York" — finds America/New_York
  • "London" — finds Europe/London
  • "UTC+5:30" or "Kolkata" — finds Asia/Kolkata (India Standard Time)

As you type, a dropdown appears with matching results showing the city name, country flag, and UTC offset. Click on the correct entry, then press the "+ Add City" button. The city will appear as a new row on the dashboard with a live clock and timeline bar.

You can add up to 10 cities at once. Duplicate cities are automatically prevented.

Step 2: Visualize and Customize Working Hours

Each city row displays three key pieces of information:

  • Live Clock — Shows the current time (HH:MM:SS), date, and UTC offset, updating every second.
  • 24-Hour Timeline Bar — A horizontal bar representing the full 24-hour day. Night hours (10 PM – 6 AM) appear darker, working hours appear in green, and the current hour is highlighted with a distinct marker.
  • Working Hours Controls — Two time inputs let you set custom start and end times for each city's workday. The default is 09:00 to 18:00, but you can adjust this for teams with non-standard hours or overnight shifts.

When you have 2 or more cities added, the timeline bars will also show overlap highlighting — a stronger green shade on hours where all cities' working hours intersect. This gives you an instant visual read on when everyone is available.

Step 3: Find the Best Meeting Time

Once you've added at least 2 cities and adjusted working hours (if needed), click the "Find Best Meeting Time" button in the Meeting Overlap Finder section.

The algorithm will:

  1. Convert each city's working hours to UTC
  2. Find all hours that fall within every city's working time
  3. Group contiguous hours into time windows
  4. Sort by duration (longest overlap first)
  5. Display each window with local times for every city

Each result card shows:

  • A quality indicator — "Perfect overlap" (4+ hours), "Limited window" (2-3 hours), or "Tight window" (1 hour)
  • The UTC time range
  • The local time for each city
  • A "Copy to clipboard" button to share the results instantly

If no overlapping hours exist, the tool will display a clear message suggesting you adjust working hours or reduce the number of cities.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a city to compare time zones?

Type the city name or IANA time zone identifier (like "America/New_York") into the search bar. Select the correct result from the dropdown, then click "+ Add City". The city will appear with a live clock and 24-hour timeline bar.

How many cities can I compare at once?

You can compare up to 10 cities simultaneously. Each city displays a live clock with hours, minutes, and seconds, the current date, UTC offset, and a visual 24-hour timeline bar showing working hours and night hours.

How does the meeting overlap finder work?

The overlap finder converts each city's working hours to UTC, finds the hours that are within working time for ALL selected cities, and groups them into contiguous time windows. Results are sorted by duration, with the longest overlap shown first. The algorithm correctly handles edge cases like overnight shifts and half-hour UTC offsets.

Does GlobalTime Sync handle Daylight Saving Time?

Yes. GlobalTime Sync uses the browser's built-in Intl API, which references the IANA time zone database. This database is regularly updated to reflect the latest Daylight Saving Time rules for every country and region, so you always see accurate times.

Is GlobalTime Sync free to use?

Yes, GlobalTime Sync is completely free with no sign-up required. All features — including city comparison, live clocks, timeline visualization, and the meeting overlap finder — are available to everyone at no cost. We sustain the project through non-intrusive advertising.

Can I customize working hours for each city?

Yes. Each city row has adjustable working hour inputs. The default is 09:00 to 18:00, but you can change the start and end times for any city to match that team's actual schedule. The tool even supports overnight shifts (e.g., 22:00 to 06:00).

What browsers are supported?

GlobalTime Sync works on all modern browsers including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Apple Safari, and Microsoft Edge. It requires JavaScript to be enabled. For the best experience, we recommend using the latest version of your browser.

Can I share the meeting time results with my team?

Absolutely. After finding the best meeting time, each result card has a "Copy to clipboard" button. Click it to copy all the local times in a formatted text block that you can paste into email, Slack, Microsoft Teams, or any messaging application.

Does GlobalTime Sync store my data?

GlobalTime Sync runs entirely in your browser. City selections and working hour preferences are stored only in your current session and are not saved to any server. We do not collect personal information, require account creation, or sell user data.

What time zones are available?

GlobalTime Sync supports all IANA time zones — over 400 entries covering every country and region in the world. This includes major metropolitan areas as well as regions with unique UTC offsets like Nepal (UTC+5:45), Iran (UTC+3:30), and the Chatham Islands (UTC+12:45).
